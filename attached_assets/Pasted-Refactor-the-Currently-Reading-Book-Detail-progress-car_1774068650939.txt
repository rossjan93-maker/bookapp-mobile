Refactor the “Currently Reading” / Book Detail progress card to make it visually cleaner and easier to understand.

Do NOT change backend logic.
Do NOT change schemas.
Do NOT add new features.
This is a presentation / hierarchy refactor only.

Goal
The reading-progress card currently contains too many competing signals and feels visually messy.
We need to simplify it so a user can instantly understand:

1. How far along they are
2. When they are likely to finish
3. What action to take next

Core principle
Do NOT show multiple competing pace interpretations at once.

Right now the card mixes:
- progress %
- page position
- finish projection
- avg pace / ppd
- goal pacing chip
- updated today
- actions

This feels cluttered and can reduce trust.

We need one clean narrative hierarchy.

Target information hierarchy

Primary:
- progress percent
- progress bar
- page position line: “Page X of Y · Z left”

Secondary:
- one finish projection line only

Supporting:
- one small status chip only (example: “On pace” / “Behind pace” / “Ahead of pace”)

Tertiary:
- updated today / updated X days ago

Actions:
- Update progress
- Finished
- DNF

Important rule:
Only ONE pace interpretation should be visible in the card.

Use this model:
- Keep finish projection
- Remove pages/day from the visible chip/line
- The chip should only communicate pacing state:
  - Ahead of pace
  - On pace
  - Behind pace

Copy requirements

Replace any copy like:
- “At your avg pace (~216 ppd): finish by Mar 22”
with cleaner language:
- “Finish by Mar 22 at your usual pace”

If current pace is the correct source instead of average pace, use:
- “Finish by Mar 22 at your current pace”

Do NOT show parentheses with ppd in the main card UI.

Do NOT show a numeric pages/day metric in this card.

Layout requirements

Refactor the card so it feels cleaner:

1. top:
- READING PROGRESS label
- large % value
- progress bar

2. under bar:
- “Page X of Y · Z left”

3. below that:
- projection line:
  - “Finish by Mar 22 at your usual pace”

4. below that:
- one compact pacing chip only:
  - Ahead of pace
  - On pace
  - Behind pace

5. below that:
- “Updated today” / “Updated 2 days ago”

6. actions at bottom:
- primary full-width: Update progress
- secondary row below:
  - Finished
  - DNF

Button hierarchy

Primary:
- Update progress

Secondary:
- Finished
- DNF

Requirements:
- Finished and DNF should have similar visual weight
- DNF should not scream red by default
- DNF can remain subtly destructive, but quieter than now
- the actions should feel grouped and intentional

Visual / spacing goals

- reduce visual crowding
- avoid competing text blocks
- preserve calm, premium feel
- keep the card scannable in 2 seconds

Validation question
After the refactor, a user should be able to answer instantly:
- How far along am I?
- When will I likely finish?
- What should I tap next?

Output
When complete, provide:
1. files changed
2. what was removed from the visible card
3. what the new hierarchy is
4. what copy changed
5. any small presentation limitations still deferred

Important
This is not a logic sprint.
Do not rework pace calculations.
Do not add more metrics.
Do not redesign unrelated screens.
Only simplify and improve the reading-progress card presentation.
