Pause further title-specific fixes.

We are concerned that we are still approaching the No-dark / lens problem too narrowly by patching specific titles or adding isolated signal phrases.

The goal is broader:
Readstack needs a holistic Book Evidence + Intent Lens Eligibility system that can evaluate any book against any temporary reader lens.

Specific titles should be validator fixtures, not the basis of product logic.

Task type:
Architecture consolidation + minimal implementation plan. Implement only if contained.

Current accepted state:
- Reading Taste = durable baseline.
- Your Next Read = temporary session lens.
- Shared evaluateBookAgainstIntentLens(...) now exists.
- No-dark should be hard exclusion only with specific evidence.
- Less-dark should be bounded demotion.
- Unknown evidence should not hard-exclude.

Concern:
The evaluator may still be relying too much on isolated phrase lists rather than a normalized book-evidence model. This risks whack-a-mole fixes for Verity, The Secret History, etc.

Goal:
Define and, if contained, implement a normalized BookEvidence layer that the evaluator consumes.

## Desired model

Create or identify a normalized evidence object for each book, derived from existing metadata and BookTraits:

- tone
- toneConfidence
- pace
- paceConfidence
- complexity
- complexityConfidence
- intensity
- intensityConfidence
- emotionalWeight
- emotionalWeightConfidence
- lengthClass
- seriesPosition
- genre/lane/market_position
- evidence phrases / supporting metadata

Do not invent certainty.
Unknown remains unknown.

## Lens policy

Map session lenses to evidence requirements:

### Hard exclusions
- No dark
- No romance
- No literary
- format-only filters
- max length
- standalone-only

Hard exclusions require specific evidence.

### Soft demotions / not-right-now
- Less dark
- light & accessible
- short & light
- low intensity
- slower/faster
- palate cleanser / low-burden mood

These should use P4C.1 bounded contribution influence, not hard exclusion unless an explicit No-X filter is selected.
