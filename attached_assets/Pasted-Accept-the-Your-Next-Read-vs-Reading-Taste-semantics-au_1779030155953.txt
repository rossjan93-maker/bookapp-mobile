Accept the “Your Next Read vs Reading Taste” semantics audit.

We are not proceeding to P4D yet.

The audit changed the interpretation of the last filter smoke:
- The “Your Next Read” filter is correctly session-only and does not mutate durable Reading Taste.
- But the filter currently bypasses P4C typed contribution plumbing.
- It uses legacy hard/exclude rules through `handleApplyIntent` / `getIntentExclusionReason`.
- Therefore the recent filter smoke validates the temporary lens behavior, not P4C.1 contribution behavior.

Task type:
Targeted Your Next Read lens semantics fix.
No composer changes. No RecCard changes. No durable storage changes.

Goal:
Make the temporary lens honest and prepare it to flow into the P4C contribution model later.

## Part A — label honesty

Update the user-facing “Your Next Read” filter labels/subtitles so they match actual behavior.

Audit findings:
- “No dark” / “No literary” / “No romance” are true hard exclusions. These labels are honest.
- “Low intensity” currently maps to `exclude.avoid_dark`, which is effectively “no dark content,” not just a low-intensity nudge.
- “Light & fun” currently maps to `avoid_dark + avoid_literary`.
- “Palate cleanser” currently maps to `avoid_dark + max_page_count=400`.

Please make labels/subtitles honest.

Possible direction:
- “Low intensity” → “Less dark” or split into “Low intensity” as a soft lens and “No dark” as a hard lens.
- “Palate cleanser” → “Short & light”
- “Light & fun” → “Light & accessible”

Do not change behavior in this Part A unless the label would otherwise become misleading.

## Part B — architecture prep, no full implementation unless very small

Add or sketch a `currentIntentLens` view-model that explicitly separates:
- hard session exclusions: No dark, No romance, No literary, max length, fiction/nonfiction-only, standalone-only
- soft session preferences: lighter, slower/faster, lower intensity, more playful, more challenging, etc.
- temporary avoids / not-right-now risks

If implementing the view-model is small and behavior-neutral, do it.
If not, document the exact implementation seam for the next batch.

Important:
The lens should remain session-only.
It must not write into durable Reading Taste.
It must not mutate `reader_preferences`.

## Part C — conflict awareness

If easy, add non-invasive instrumentation or a placeholder hook to detect when the session lens hides books matching stated durable favorites.

Example:
- user’s Reading Taste includes Romance

- app should eventually be able to say: “Hiding romance picks while this lens is active.”

Do not build the UI yet unless trivial.
Document the seam.
