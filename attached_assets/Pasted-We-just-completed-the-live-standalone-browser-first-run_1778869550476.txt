However, the live product experience exposed a first-session state-honesty problem:

Observed sequence:
1. User completes the quick taste check.
2. Taste Readout shows personalized summary chips, e.g.:
   - Reading for: surprise
   - Tone: darker
   - unusual premises
   - Less of: Fantasy
3. User taps “See my picks.”
4. For You opens, but shows:
   - “Popular starting points · Not personalized yet”
   - generic seeded books
   - the “Let’s set up your shelf” card still offering:
     - Import your library
     - Add a few
     - Quick quiz

This is visibly inconsistent. The user just completed the quick quiz and was told they were about to see their picks, but the destination treats them as not personalized and offers the same quick quiz again.

- The non-import cold start should ultimately become a higher-signal intelligence-gathering experience, not merely a taxonomy-first quiz.
- The likely future direction includes richer book-native anchors, expressive “why this worked / didn’t work” signal, sharper ambiguity-resolving questions, a stronger Reader Thesis / Taste Readout, and a more opinionated first recommendation deck.
- Therefore, do not use this investigation to polish or expand the current quiz architecture.
- The task here is only to diagnose whether the current flow has a state/persistence/handoff bug or an intentional-but-now-misleading product rule that makes the post-quiz landing experience dishonest.
