ISSUE 1 — Empty recommendations page for new users

Problem:
The empty recommendations / low-signal page feels poorly framed.
The heading sits too high and the layout feels unfinished.

Task:
Improve the empty/low-signal state on the For You screen.

Requirements:
- better vertical framing
- clearer explanation of why recommendations are not ready yet
- strong next action
- avoid making the user feel like the app is broken
- support users who skipped import or have very little reading data

Acceptance criteria:
- new users understand what to do next
- page does not feel empty or broken
- CTA hierarchy is clear

---

ISSUE 2 — For You heading/subheading copy

Problem:
The current For You copy is redundant and awkward.
We repeat “For You” and “high confidence recommendation” too much.

Task:
Rewrite the For You header and subheader so they are cleaner and more natural.

Direction:
- remove repeated “For You” language
- remove repeated “high confidence recommendation” phrasing
- keep the idea of personalized picks
- make the language feel confident but not robotic

Acceptance criteria:
- heading and subheading are grammatically clean
- no duplicated idea in adjacent lines
- copy feels premium and human

---

ISSUE 3 — App-wide green color consistency

Problem:
The app uses too many shades of green.

Task:
Audit current green usage and consolidate around one primary green with intentional tints.

Requirements:
- define one primary green token
- define limited supporting green tint(s)
- replace inconsistent one-off greens where practical
- do not flatten every color blindly; preserve contrast and accessibility

Acceptance criteria:
- buttons, progress bars, badges, highlights, and success states feel visually consistent
- no obvious competing greens on the same screen
- color choices are centralized if possible

---

ISSUE 4 — Motion and fluidity

Problem:
The app feels too static.
We need subtle motion and reactive feedback, especially around progress, scroll, reveal, and streaks.

Task:
Add tasteful motion where it improves product feel.

Examples:
- Reading streak flame should feel alive when a streak exists
- progress numbers can animate gently when changed
- progress bar can fill on mount
- cards can reveal subtly on screen load
- tap/selection states should feel responsive

Constraints:
- do not over-animate
- no gimmicky animations
- respect performance
- keep motion subtle and premium

Acceptance criteria:
- app feels more fluid
- key elements respond to user action
- no distracting or excessive animation
- no degraded scroll performance

---

ISSUE 5 — Reading Progress section polish

Problem:
The Reading Progress section is directionally improved but still needs refinement.

Task:
Polish layout, spacing, and hierarchy.

Requirements:
- keep the combined Reading Progress model
- activity metrics, yearly goal, and completed books should feel like one cohesive section
- ensure the activity row always shows a streak slot, even at 0
- goal progress and pacing should feel visually connected
- completed books shelf should feel grounded, not floating
- left-aligned and editorial in tone

Acceptance criteria:
- section feels cohesive rather than like stacked unrelated widgets
- hierarchy is clear
- spacing feels intentional on mobile
- completed book row looks polished and scrollable
