Task type:
Audit / product-copy diagnosis only.

Goal:
Improve the quality of recommendation explanation language shown on For You cards and related recommendation surfaces.

User-reported issue:
Recommendation descriptions/reasons still read awkwardly and do not feel like strong decision support. Examples seen in the app:
- “Strong match for prose and emotional.”
- “Anchored in prose and emotional.”
- “Prose and emotional comes through clearly.”

These phrases are technically tied to internal trait logic, but they do not sound natural, useful, or premium to a reader.

We need to audit the full explanation-copy pipeline before changing anything.

Do not implement yet.

Allowed files to inspect:
- components/RecCard.tsx
- components/RecommendationsFeed.tsx
- lib/expertRec.ts
- lib/recommender.ts
- lib/tasteProfile.ts
- lib/evidencePack.ts if it contributes to recommendation reasons
- any helper used by buildExplanation / rewriteReasonText / anchored explanations
- any source that generates reasons[] or explanation text for rec cards

Forbidden:
- Do not edit code.
- Do not touch schema/migrations.
- Do not change scoring/ranking/retrieval.
- Do not change recommendation persistence or queues.
- Do not touch auth/Sentry/native/Open Library/Google Books/metadata/LLM.
- Do not broaden into Taste Readout, onboarding, Home, or filters.

Audit questions:

1. Current explanation pipeline
Trace the full path for recommendation reasons:
- where raw reasons originate
- how reasons[] are generated
- how RecCard chooses which explanation to display
- where rewriteReasonText, buildAnchoredExplanation, trait pools, and fallback pools sit in the hierarchy
- when raw source text survives versus gets rewritten

2. Root cause of awkward phrases
Identify exactly which function(s) produce phrases like:
- “Strong match for prose and emotional.”
- “Anchored in prose and emotional.”
- “Prose and emotional comes through clearly.”

Determine whether the issue is:
- internal trait labels being concatenated too literally
- poor phrase pools
- fallback copy firing too often
- strong evidence being expressed weakly
- mismatched singular/plural grammar
- traits that need humanization before sentence assembly

3. Explanation quality taxonomy
Classify current explanation paths into:
- strong / worth keeping
- safe but bland
- awkward or product-damaging
- overclaiming risk
- redundant/repetitive

4. What a good explanation should do
Recommend a clear copy standard for rec card explanations.

Examples of the desired direction:
- “A strong fit if you want literary writing with real emotional weight.”
- “This leans into the reflective, character-heavy side of your profile.”
- “Starting from your interest in darker, faster-moving stories, this is a reasonable early fit.”
- “A more literary pick from the same suspense lane you’ve been steering toward.”

We want:
- natural English
- useful decision context
- clear confidence level
- groundedness to available evidence
- no internal-label feel

5. Safe copy by evidence level
Propose explanation treatment for:
- intake-only / thin profile users
- users with stated preferences + a little activity
- history-rich users
- repeated author/lane evidence
- generic fallback when no meaningful personalization exists

6. Specific trait language improvements
Audit how these kinds of trait combinations are rendered today:
- prose + emotional
- characters + emotional
- pacing + insight
- depth + originality
- genre + tone
- genre + reading outcome

Recommend how they should be humanized.

7. Proposed implementation path
Recommend the smallest high-value improvement batch.

Likely possibilities:
- repair trait-humanization pools inside RecCard copy helpers
- stop emitting clunky raw trait-pair strings
- introduce a small set of curated phrase builders for common trait combinations
- improve fallback reason rewrite logic
- preserve V3 anchored explanation trust gating

Do not propose scoring changes unless absolutely necessary; this should ideally be copy-layer work only.

Break into small batches if needed:
- FX-1: explanation-copy root fix
