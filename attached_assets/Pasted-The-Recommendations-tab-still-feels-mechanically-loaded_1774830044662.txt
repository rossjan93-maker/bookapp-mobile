The Recommendations tab still feels mechanically loaded and visually jumpy. I want a combined fix for entry-state UX and action-state UX.

Observed problems from the recordings:
1. On tab open, the screen can show both:
   - a top-level entry loader (“Finding your next picks...”)
   - and a secondary refresh card (“Refreshing your picks...”)
   This feels like dual loading.

2. When I act on recommendation cards:
   - the stack reflows abruptly
   - cards disappear too suddenly
   - lower sections like “Refine your taste” jump upward
   - the page feels like it is statically re-rendering instead of smoothly transitioning

3. Action feedback is inconsistent:
   - “Not for me” shows as a slim dismiss/undo chip style
   - “Want to Read” shows as a large green confirmation block
   - these do not feel like part of one interaction system

4. After acting on cards, the screen sometimes falls back into a visible loading/reset state instead of smoothly advancing the next recommendation into place.

I want this fixed at the state-model and motion-system level, not with scattered cosmetic patches.

Goals:
A) Recommendations tab should feel immediate on open
B) Acting on cards should feel smooth, continuous, and premium
C) Only one loading / refreshing / exhausted treatment should ever be visible at once
D) Lower modules should not jump abruptly when the recommendation stack changes

Please inspect the Recommend tab lifecycle and refactor toward a single display-state model plus consistent card-transition behavior.

What I want behaviorally:

TAB ENTRY
1. If a usable deck already exists:
   - show the deck immediately on tab open
   - do background refresh silently or with only a very subtle inline status
   - do NOT show full loading again
   - do NOT show both entry loading and refresh card together

2. If no usable deck exists yet:
   - show exactly one loading treatment
   - do NOT stack multiple loaders

3. If deck is exhausted:
   - show exactly one exhausted/caught-up state
   - do NOT stack it with loaders unless a true retry is happening

CARD ACTIONS
4. When I tap “Want to Read”, “Not for me”, or “More like this”:
   - the card should enter a temporary action-confirmed state
   - then glide out / collapse smoothly
   - the next card should move up with animation, not a static jump

5. The top recommendation area should maintain structural stability:
   - preserve container height during transition where appropriate
   - avoid the whole page shifting abruptly upward
   - “Refine your taste” and other lower modules should move smoothly, ideally with layout animation

6. Use one consistent action-feedback system:
   - unify “Want to Read” and “Not for me” interaction language and visual pattern
   - feedback can vary by tone/color, but the structure should be consistent
   - avoid one action producing a tiny chip while another produces a large block unless intentionally designed as the same component family

7. Do not wipe the whole recommendation area just because one card was acted on.
   - Prefer optimistic local deck mutation
   - Then background reconcile with server/session state
   - Keep the deck feeling continuous
