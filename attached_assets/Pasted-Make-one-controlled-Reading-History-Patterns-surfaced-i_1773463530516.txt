Primary product objectives

A. Add a Reading Patterns section to Profile
Requirements:
1. Add a new section in Profile called something like:
   - Reading Patterns
   - Your Reading Patterns
   - Reading History
2. This should feel editorial and compact, not like a spreadsheet.
3. Place it in a sensible position in Profile, likely below Reader Insights and above lower-priority admin/social sections.

B. Surface a small set of credible patterns
Requirements:
1. Use the current data foundation to derive a few high-value patterns.
2. Good candidates include:
   - self-added vs recommended mix
   - completion vs DNF tendency
   - average time-to-finish when enough data exists
   - most common genres/subjects when enough metadata exists
   - author repeat behavior if enough repetition exists
3. You do NOT need to show all of these.
4. Prefer 3–5 strong patterns over a noisy list.
5. Only show patterns when there is enough data to support them.

C. Data sufficiency rules
Requirements:
1. Add clear threshold gating so the app does not overstate patterns from tiny samples.
2. If there is not enough data, show a graceful placeholder such as:
   - “As you finish more books, your reading patterns will take shape.”
   - “We’re still learning how you read.”
3. Keep all pattern claims honest and grounded in actual captured data.

D. Use existing signals where appropriate
Requirements:
1. Extend lib/signals.ts if needed.
2. Keep the logic analyzable and explicit.
3. Do not build AI summaries or speculative personality labels.
4. Do not invent signals the app does not truly have.

E. UI style
Requirements:
1. Keep the current editorial reading-app design language:
   - warm neutrals
   - strong hierarchy
   - compact premium cards/tiles/rows
2. Avoid dashboard clutter.
3. Prefer readable human phrasing over analyst jargon.
4. Good examples:
   - “Most of your finished books have come from your own library.”
   - “You finish about 3 out of every 4 books you start.”
   - “You tend to finish recommended books less often than self-picked ones.”
   - “You’ve been returning to literary fiction most often.”
5. Keep copy grounded in actual data and thresholds.
