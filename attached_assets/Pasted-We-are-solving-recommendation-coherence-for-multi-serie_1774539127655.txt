We are solving recommendation coherence for multi-series universes (e.g. Robin Hobb, Sanderson, Maas).

Do NOT change:
- exclusion logic
- contiguous logic
- recommendation scoring weights broadly
- UI layout yet

We are introducing a new concept:
SAGA GROUPING

Goal
Treat multiple related series as a single coherent reading journey.

Example:
Realm of the Elderlings is not 5 independent series.
It is one saga with ordered sub-series.

The system must:
- understand this structure
- recommend appropriately within it
- explain it clearly

---

Part 1 — Define saga registry

Extend seriesCatalog.ts with a new structure:

Each saga must include:
- saga_name
- ordered list of series (not books)
- each series already maps to ordered books (already exists)

Example structure:

Realm of the Elderlings:
  [
    Farseer Trilogy,
    Liveship Traders,
    Tawny Man,
    Rain Wild Chronicles,
    Fitz and the Fool
  ]

Mistborn:
  [
    Era 1,
    Era 2
  ]

Do NOT infer this dynamically.
This must be explicit, curated, and deterministic.

---

Part 2 — Compute saga progress

For each user:
- determine which series in the saga are:
  - completed
  - in progress
  - not started

Produce:
- saga_max_series_index
- saga_next_series
- saga_is_sequential (similar concept to contiguous, but at series level)

---

Part 3 — Recommendation gating

Update recommendation behavior:

Rule A — If user is mid-saga:
- only recommend:
  - next book in current series
  - OR first book of next series (if current series complete)

Rule B — Do NOT recommend:
- books from later series in the saga (skip-ahead at saga level)

Rule C — If user has not started saga:
- only recommend first entry point of saga (Book 1 of Series 1)

---

Part 4 — Explanation update

When a book is part of a saga:

Replace current series explanation with:

If continuing:
  "Continue your journey in [Saga Name]"

If next series:
  "Start the next chapter of [Saga Name]"

If entry point:
  "[Saga Name] begins here"

Do NOT mention internal series names unless necessary.

---

Part 5 — Do NOT change UI yet

No visual changes.
No new components.
Only:
- data model
- recommendation eligibility
- explanation layer

---

Return:

1. Objective

5. Recommendation rules (explicit logic)
6. Before vs after examples (use Robin Hobb specifically)
7. Live proof (show what is now suppressed vs allowed)

Success condition

For a user like the Goodreads tester:
- The app never recommends Book 14 of a saga without context
- Recommendations feel like a guided journey, not random entries
- Robin Hobb recommendations become intuitive and explainable
