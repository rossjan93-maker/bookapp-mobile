Important rule

Goal
Make the deterministic free recommendation engine credible and truthy for dense imported users even before premium expert reasoning.

What to change

Part 1: Add a dense-import deterministic mode
For users with large imported history, switch the free engine to a stricter deterministic mode that prioritizes:
- repeated authors
- repeated high-rated authors
- repeated series completion
- repeated high-rated subgenre clusters
- modern commercial reading patterns

This mode should not behave like the current broad trait/genre blend.

Part 2: Lane-first deterministic retrieval
Build deterministic lanes from repeated reading behavior.

For this kind of user, likely lanes are:
- romantasy / emotional fantasy
- contemporary emotional/book-club fiction
- modern suspense / thriller / puzzle mystery
- memoir / narrative nonfiction

Retrieve candidates within those lanes first, then rank across them.

Do NOT let broad literary/canon spillover dominate retrieval.

Part 3: Strong exception handling
Implement explicit logic:
- isolated highly rated classics are exceptions, not dominant preferences
- a few literary/canon titles do not justify Hemingway/Ayn Rand style recommendations
- memoir tolerance does not justify spiritual memoir/philosophy leakage
- Horowitz-style mystery does not justify hard-boiled noir by default

Part 4: Add a modern commercial prior in free mode
If the user repeatedly reads modern commercially successful fiction with:
- relationship/emotional stakes
- readable prose
- series depth
- suspense/book-club crossover patterns

Then free mode should bias toward books with similar market position and reading feel.

This should down-rank:
- classic/public-domain/canon spillover
- older male literary drift
- philosophical/spiritual drift
- hard-boiled noir unless explicitly supported

Part 5: Rework mystery handling
Do not treat all mystery as equivalent.
Separate:
- contemporary suspense/domestic thriller
- puzzle/meta-detective mystery
- hard-boiled noir

A user liking Anthony Horowitz and Lucy Foley should not automatically get Raymond Chandler.

Part 6: Better deterministic explanations
Free-mode explanations should stop using overly generic lines like:
- “Aligns with your preference for characters and ending”
- “Matches your appreciation for originality”

Instead use lane-aware deterministic language like:
- “Feels adjacent to the fantasy series you repeatedly complete”
- “Fits your pattern of emotionally driven contemporary fiction”
- “Matches the twisty, readable suspense you rate highly”
- “Sits closer to your strongest recurring authors than to your one-off exceptions”

Part 7: Hard penalties
In free mode, add stronger penalties for:
- canon/classic drift when classics are not a repeated lane
- public-domain spillover
- philosophy/spiritual drift
- hard-boiled noir when user mystery preference is modern/commercial
- recommendations driven mainly by isolated exception books

Part 8: Debug output
For dense imported users, show in debug mode:
- detected deterministic lanes
- repeated authors used
- repeated series used
- whether a classic/canon penalty was applied
- whether a recommendation came from dominant-lane evidence or exception leakage
