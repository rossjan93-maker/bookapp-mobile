The Reading Insights page is improving, but it still is not impactful enough.

Right now it is mostly describing what happened:
- pages read
- reading days
- a rhythm chart
- a few stats

That is not enough.

I want this page to move from raw analytics into interpretation, reflection, and gentle guidance.

Core goal
Make the Reading Insights page answer a bigger question than “what happened?”
It should help the user feel things like:
- I’m building momentum
- I understand my reading habits
- I’m doing better than I thought
- this app understands me as a reader
- I know what to do next

New direction
Shift the page from “stat dashboard” toward “interpreted reading intelligence.”

What I want changed
1. Add a strong interpretive headline at the top of the month view
Examples of the kind of thing I mean:
- A strong start to April
- Reading is light, but momentum is building
- One deep session shaped your month
- Your rhythm is still forming
Do not hardcode these exact lines. Generate them from real data and the state of the month.

2. Add a short interpretive subline beneath the hero metric
This should explain what the month currently means, not just restate the number.
Examples:
- 100 pages across 1 reading day, mostly driven by The Two Towers
- Your reading month is early, but you’ve already made meaningful progress
- Most of this month’s momentum is coming from one focused session

3. Make the rhythm chart say something
The chart is currently the strongest visual element, but it still feels descriptive instead of insightful.
I want a short insight paired with or just below it, such as:
- You tend to read in concentrated bursts
- Reading has been light but consistent
- Your pace usually picks up later in the month
- This month is being shaped by a few focused sessions
The insight should be generated from real session behavior, not random flavor text.

4. Add one calm, useful next-step prompt
Not productivity-guilt. Not “read X pages today.”
Something softer and more helpful, for example:
- Another reading day would start a streak
- At this pace, you could finish The Two Towers by Apr 19
- You’ve made strong progress on one book — now is a good time to keep momentum
- Trends are still forming this early in the month
This should feel like quiet guidance, not pressure.

5. Tie insights back to reader identity where appropriate
When the data supports it, the page should help the user understand what kind of reader they are being this month.
Examples:
- You’re reading in focused, high-page sessions
- This month is being shaped by one deep read
- Your reading style is steady rather than frequent
Again: no hardcoded lines. Generate from real data.

6. Reduce equal-weight stat clutter
Do not keep adding more metrics just because the data exists.
Every visible stat or sentence must earn its place by changing meaning, not just adding information.
If needed, simplify the stat area and give more room to the interpretive layer.

7. Preserve the product tone
This should feel:
- reflective
- calm
- premium
- gently encouraging
- literary/editorial

It should not feel:
- like a productivity app
- like a dashboard
- like a fitness tracker
- like homework
- fake-celebratory

Scope
Focus on the month view first.
Only improve the year view if the logic naturally carries over cleanly.
Do not overbuild a giant narrative engine. Keep the logic understandable and grounded in the actual data model we already have.

What I want back
1. exact files changed
2. what interpretive logic or insight-generation rules were added
3. before/after examples using real month states
4. screenshots or visual proof
5. what you intentionally simplified or removed
6. what you would improve next if we keep pushing this page
